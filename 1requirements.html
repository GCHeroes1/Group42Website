Requirements

Project background

A previous IXN MSc project created a chatbot with the aim of improving how GPs can access educational material. It was presented to the leading GPs at the RCGP and sparked lots of interest! We’d like to continue to develop and build out this chatbot focusing on a larger and more complex educational resource.

Requirement gathering

In order to design our application, we held semi-structured interviews with multiple end-users (GPs) to see their opinion on the direction the project should take; below is an example of one of these interviews that gave us a lot of relevant data points. Unstructured questions that were added during the interview are highlighted in blue.

Q: What are your thoughts on the current system used by the NHS for getting medical information and documents?

A: The current GP system we have just pulls up documents and we search through… it works fine but it can take a while looking through all the information because of how big the files are.

Q: If you could improve the system in some way, what improvement would you make?

A: I’d speed up the process of looking through the documents somehow. Would save GPs in my clinic a lot of time.

Q: Have you used an example of AI before and if so, in what context?

A: I have an Amazon Alexa at home and I use it for simple things like playing music and setting alarms. Sometimes I use it for random questions and facts too.

Q: So when asking your Alexa these questions on occasion, how do you feel about the response you get back? Does it answer your question, and if not how do you react?

A: Usually Alexa will answer the question perfectly, but sometimes she responds saying she doesn’t know how to answer. Usually when that happens I just ignore it and search it on my phone instead- her answering it is more for convenience so I don’t have to check myself.

Q: How would you feel about the ability to ask an AI (like Alexa) to get the medical information you get from documents in the current NHS system for you?

A: Alexa? (we clarified at this point that the AI was a text based chatbot, not speech based) In that case my biggest issue is that I am trusting the AI to return to me the correct answer- what if it returns an answer from the wrong document, and because I can’t check it I endanger a patient? That would put me off heavily.

Q: Following on from the issue you highlighted, what if you could see the document it took the data from?

A: That would put my mind at ease, I can get to things faster and double check that it is from the right source. As long as there is an alternative to search manually, I see it as a win/win.

Q: Finally, where would you want to find this AI to talk to?

A: As long as it is accessible to us during appointments at our computer. I work at a local clinic so I don’t move from my room much, however GPs in hospitals move around a lot, so to them the need to be mobile is important. Maybe as a mobile app for convenience?

Personas

Scenarios

Scenario 1:

Harold is a very experienced GP however he still has some gaps in his knowledge especially in regards to less medical specific information such as patient safeguarding. He had a potentially vulnerable patient coming in to see him and wanted to check that he’d be acting in accordance with the safeguarding protocols in place, so he pulled up the chatbot through an IM application on his computer.

He found interacting with an IM interface very intuitive and user friendly. He also preferred the colloquial format of talking to an AI much better than having to look through documents to find the same information. As the chatbot can be integrated into multiple IM applications, Harold appreciated how easy the application was to access within his own IM and that the security of the IM extended to the messages exchanged with the chatbot. He was able to gain information from the chatbot and his message exchanges were saved on his IM account for him to read later.

Scenario 2:

Elon is training to be a GP and wants to expand his knowledge on certain medical areas whilst at home to help him with his training, however he finds it hard to look through long lists of documents with undescriptive titles to try and find the exact information he needs, so he decided to consult our chatbot in order to make this process easier.

Through an internet enabled device, he was able to query our chatbot through the online chat interface and receive a snapshot of a document, containing the information he was requesting. He was also able to save a copy of the full document in case he wishes to find more similar information in the future or to check whether the information from the chatbot was in the correct context.

He really liked that he was able to find specific information without necessarily having the knowledge of the broader field and document it is contained in. He also found being given the full document to read later very helpful.

Project goals

The basis of our project is to provide information about medical sector projects to academics, medical professionals and any other individuals, in a way that doesn't require trawling the web or a technical background to use. The key goals of this is for:

  1. Future institutions partaking in the IXN scheme to organise their projects into a single database
  2. Encourage entrepreneurs in the medical sector to invest in their ideas, by providing better market information
  3. Improve the quality of projects in development by sharing information between researchers on progress of similar projects

MoSCoW requirement list

MUST HAVE

  • The ability to ask a chatbot questions about projects and have it return a list that matches the user’s criteria - keywords like FHIR, specific devices. The ability to see where in the country it was done and it’s availability (github links). It should also have the project status.

  • A database of IXN project data that the user can search through

  • A means of pushing projects to the database through the administrator panel

  • Projects returned by the chatbot that display information on:

    • Project stage (how far along in development it is in)

    • The location and partnered organization

    • The students and employees involved in the project

    • The abstract and information on the project title

    • Devices, skills and topics used in the proiect

  • A suggestion for similar projects in the event of a no match for a chatbot query

  • A Q&A system for AI related questions

  • Provide links to external resources (tutorials, videos etc.) related to the user’s question

  • Organisation of projects: related projects, with a date field that shows which university worked on what.

SHOULD HAVE

  • Ability for local storage of database so academic institutes can host it for their own projects

  • National database linking all databases together

  • A means of updating existing projects through the administrator panel

  • The ability to add multimedia (pptx, photos and videos) to project entries and access these from queries in the chatbot

  • Integrate user authentication with major accounts such as Google or Facebook

  • Prompted responses to chatbot messages

  • Ability to allow other databases to be used for information retrieval e.g. When a search happens see if there is any related articles or pubmed publications

COULD HAVE

  • Provide summarised data on the company that has given IXN form data and related academic research papers for the user to open and read

  • Ability to categorise tags manually projects into integration projects, efficiency projects and innovation projects

  • Visualisation of the progress of a project (indicating if it has matured or stagnated etc.) - project status update

  • Integration with the BNF (Medical Database of Pharmaceuticals) to check the legal status of project specifications e.g. status of drugs used in a project

  • Projects returned by the chatbot that display information on:

    • NDA, Ethics agreements and contracts related to the projects

  • Plot keyword search results geographically

WONT HAVE

  • Answers to non-AI related questions that users would ask
